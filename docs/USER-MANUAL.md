## Getting Started

### Your First Conversation

1. **Log in** to HolaHola with your account
2. **Select a language** you want to learn
3. **Start a conversation** by clicking "Call Tutor" or typing in the chat
4. **Speak naturally** - the AI tutor adapts to your level

### Supported Languages

HolaHola supports 9 languages:
- Spanish, French, German, Italian, Portuguese
- Japanese, Mandarin Chinese, Korean
- English (for non-native speakers)

### Learning Modes

| Mode | Best For |
|------|----------|
| **Voice Chat** | Pronunciation, conversation practice, immersive learning |
| **Text Chat** | Reading, writing practice, reviewing at your own pace |
| **Drill Mode** | Focused practice on vocabulary and numbers |

## Voice Learning & Whiteboard

During voice lessons, your AI tutor displays visual teaching aids on a "whiteboard" to help you learn.

### What You'll See

The tutor uses these tools strategically - not all at once, but when they're most helpful:

#### Core Tools

| Tool | What It Does |
|------|--------------|
| **Text Display** | Shows vocabulary words and key phrases prominently |
| **Pronunciation** | Breaks down difficult words into syllables with stress |
| **Corrections** | Shows the correct form alongside what was incorrect |
| **Images** | Displays pictures to reinforce vocabulary |

#### Interactive Tools

| Tool | What It Does |
|------|--------------|
| **Exercises** | Interactive drills (repeat, translate, fill-in-the-blank, matching) |
| **Audio Replay** | Button to hear pronunciation again at different speeds |
| **Word Map** | Shows related words (synonyms, antonyms, word families) |

#### Context Tools

| Tool | What It Does |
|------|--------------|
| **Example Sentences** | Shows a word used in multiple real sentences |
| **Grammar Tables** | Verb conjugation charts for any tense |
| **Cultural Tips** | Information about customs, etiquette, and culture |
| **Scenarios** | Sets up role-play situations (café, train station, etc.) |
| **Lesson Summary** | Recap of vocabulary and phrases learned |

### Audio Replay

When the tutor shows a phrase with a play button, you can:
- Click to hear the pronunciation
- Choose speed: **Slow** (0.5x), **Normal** (1x), or **Fast** (1.5x)
- Replay as many times as you need

### Role-Play Scenarios

The tutor may set up immersive practice situations:
- **Location**: Where the scene takes place (café, office, etc.)
- **Situation**: What's happening and what you need to do
- **Mood**: The tone of the conversation (formal, casual, urgent)

Practice responding as if you were really there!

## Interactive Exercises

During lessons, you'll encounter practice exercises to reinforce learning.

### Listen & Repeat

**What It Is:** Hear a phrase and repeat it back.

**How It Works:**
1. The tutor displays the phrase
2. Click play to hear pronunciation
3. Speak the phrase
4. Get feedback on your pronunciation

**Tips:**
- Use slow speed first for difficult phrases
- Focus on rhythm and intonation
- Don't worry about perfection - practice builds skill

### Translation

**What It Is:** Translate a word or phrase to your target language.

**How It Works:**
1. See a prompt asking how to say something
2. Think of the translation
3. Say or type your answer
4. Get feedback

**Tips:**
- Try to answer before looking at hints
- Practice both directions (English ↔ target language)

### Fill in the Blank (December 2025)

**What It Is:** Complete a sentence with the missing word using dropdown or text input.

**Two Input Modes:**

**Dropdown Mode:**
1. See a sentence with a blank and a dropdown menu
2. Click the dropdown to see options
3. Select the correct word
4. Get instant feedback (green ✓ or try again)

**Text Input Mode:**
1. See a sentence with a blank text field
2. Type the missing word
3. Click "Check" or press Enter
4. Correct answer shown if needed

**Tips:**
- Read the whole sentence first
- Consider grammar rules
- Check verb conjugations
- Case doesn't matter for text input

### Sentence Order (December 2025)

**What It Is:** Put scrambled words in the correct order to form a sentence.

**How It Works:**
1. See a set of scrambled word tiles
2. Arrange them in the correct order:
   - **Drag and drop** the tiles, OR
   - **Click tiles** in order to build the sentence
3. Submit your answer
4. Get feedback with the correct order shown if needed

**Tips:**
- Start with the subject or first word
- Look for verb position patterns
- Remember adjective placement rules
- Use word endings as clues

### Matching

**What It Is:** Match words with their translations.

**How It Works:**
1. See two columns of items
2. Click an item on the left
3. Click the matching item on the right
4. Continue until all pairs are matched

**Visual Feedback:**
- **Cyan highlight:** Selected item
- **Green:** Correct match
- **Red shake:** Incorrect - try again!

**Tips:**
- Scan all options before starting
- Start with items you're confident about
- Use elimination for uncertain pairs

### Drill Mode Lessons

For intensive practice, request "drill mode" focused entirely on exercises:

- **Number Dictation:** Hear numbers and type them - great for real-world prep
- **Vocabulary Drills:** Rapid-fire translation and matching
- **Grammar Drills:** Focused verb conjugation and sentence structure practice

## Asian Language Features

Special tools for learning Japanese, Mandarin Chinese, and Korean.

### Reading Guides

When learning new characters, you'll see pronunciation guides:

**Japanese - Furigana:**
Small hiragana above kanji characters
```
食べる
たべる
(taberu - to eat)
```

**Mandarin - Pinyin:**
Tone-marked romanization above characters
```
你好
nǐ hǎo
(hello)
```

**Korean - Romanization:**
Pronunciation guide below Hangul
```
감사합니다
gamsahamnida
(thank you)
```

### Animated Stroke Order

Learn to write characters correctly with animated demonstrations:

1. **Watch the animation** - See each stroke drawn in order
2. **Note the stroke count** - Badge shows total strokes
3. **Practice along** - Trace with your finger, then on paper
4. **Replay** - Use the button to review as needed

**Why Stroke Order Matters:**
- Helps you remember characters
- Characters look right when written properly
- Builds speed with good habits
- Shows respect for the writing system

### Character Practice Tips

**Daily Practice (10 minutes):**
1. Learn 3 new characters with stroke order
2. Review 10 previously learned characters
3. Write each 3 times on paper
4. Use matching drills for vocabulary

**Common Challenges:**

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Can't remember characters | Daily stroke order practice + spaced repetition |
| Mix up similar characters | Compare side-by-side, focus on stroke differences |
| Tones are difficult (Mandarin) | Use slow speed audio, practice tone pairs |
| Reading without guides is hard | Gradually reduce reliance, focus on high-frequency characters |

### Learning Path by Level

**Beginners (ACTFL Novice):**
- Focus on reading guides to connect sounds to characters
- Master common characters first
- Build good stroke order habits early
- Use images for visual associations

**Intermediate (ACTFL Intermediate):**
- See characters in context sentences
- Build vocabulary networks with word maps
- Test knowledge with matching drills
- Try reading without pronunciation guides

**Advanced (ACTFL Advanced):**
- Read without pronunciation help
- Understand cultural usage nuances
- Learn rare or literary characters
- Focus on beautiful, correct strokes

## Vocabulary & Flashcards

### Building Your Vocabulary

During conversations, the tutor automatically extracts vocabulary for you to review. You can also add words manually.

### Spaced Repetition

HolaHola uses spaced repetition to optimize your review schedule:
- Words you know well appear less frequently
- Difficult words appear more often
- The system adapts to your performance

### Export Options

Export your vocabulary for offline study:

| Format | Best For |
|--------|----------|
| **CSV** | Spreadsheets (Excel, Google Sheets) |
| **Anki** | Anki flashcard app (tab-separated) |

**Exported Fields:**
- Word (target language)
- Translation (English)
- Pronunciation (if available)
- Example sentences
- Date added
- Last reviewed

## Grammar Practice

### Grammar Hub

Access structured grammar practice organized by topic and proficiency level.

### How It Works

1. **Choose a topic** - Verb conjugation, noun/adjective agreement, etc.
2. **Review the explanation** - Understand the grammar rule
3. **Practice exercises** - Apply what you learned
4. **Track progress** - See your mastery level

### Grammar in Conversation

During voice lessons, the tutor may show:
- **Conjugation tables** for verbs you're learning
- **Context sentences** showing grammar patterns in use
- **Corrections** when you make grammatical errors

- **Conversation time** - Minutes of practice
- **Proficiency progress** - Your ACTFL level advancement
- **Streak** - Consecutive days of practice
- **Topics covered** - What you've learned

### Viewing Progress

Check your progress in the Dashboard to see:
- Weekly activity summary
- Vocabulary growth charts
- Proficiency breakdown by skill
- Achievements and milestones

### Learning Pace Card (December 2025)

On larger screens, your dashboard sidebar shows a Learning Pace card with:
- **Lessons completed** - Total lessons finished
- **Total time learned** - Cumulative practice time
- **Current streak** - Days of consecutive practice
- **8-week activity sparkline** - Visual trend of your weekly learning
- **Average minutes per lesson** - Your typical session length

## Class Enrollment

If you're learning through a class (school, university, etc.):

### Joining a Class

1. Get a **join code** from your teacher (6 characters)
2. Go to **Classes** in the sidebar
3. Click **Join Class**
4. Enter the code and confirm

### Class Features

| Feature | What It Does |
|---------|--------------|
| **Syllabus** | See your curriculum and what's coming next |
| **Assignments** | View and complete teacher assignments |
| **Progress** | Track completion against class requirements |
| **Early Completion** | Get credit for topics covered in conversation practice |

### Asking Your Tutor About Class

During conversations, you can ask:
- "What's next in my class?"
- "Do I have any assignments due?"
- "How am I doing in Spanish 101?"

The tutor knows your class progress and can answer!

### Early Completion

If you naturally cover class topics during practice conversations:
- The system detects what you've learned
- You may qualify for early completion
- Your teacher reviews and approves
- Skip redundant lessons and move forward!

## Tips for Success

1. **Practice daily** - Short, consistent sessions beat occasional long ones
2. **Speak out loud** - Voice practice builds real fluency
3. **Engage with drills** - Active practice reinforces learning
4. **Use replay** - Hear pronunciations multiple times
5. **Learn from mistakes** - Wrong answers are opportunities
6. **Explore culture** - Understanding context helps language stick
7. **Ask questions** - The tutor adapts to your needs
